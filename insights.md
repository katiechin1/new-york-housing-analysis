# Key Insights — NY Housing Market Analysis

This analysis evaluates price patterns, affordability, and property characteristics across New York City’s five boroughs using listing data. The findings highlight where housing value differs most sharply and how property features affect pricing.

## Borough-Level Pricing
- **Manhattan** dominates both total price and price per square foot.
- **Brooklyn** displays a balanced market between high-end and mid-tier properties.
- **Bronx** and **Staten Island** remain the most affordable boroughs.

## Affordability (Price per Square Foot)
- Manhattan has the highest cost per sq ft, followed by Brooklyn.
- Staten Island and Bronx listings provide the best value per dollar.
- The affordability gap between Manhattan and outer boroughs exceeds 200% on average.

## Property Size & Value Relationship
- Price rises strongly with property size up to ~5,000 sq ft.
- Beyond that, price growth flattens—diminishing returns for luxury-scale homes.

## Bedrooms & Bathrooms
- Median price increases steadily from 1 → 4 bedrooms, then plateaus.
- Similar pattern for bathrooms: sharp rise up to ~3 baths, then slower growth.

## Property Type Patterns
- Condos and Townhouses command the highest median prices.
- Co-ops and Multi-Family homes are more affordable but show wider price variation.
- Non-representative listing types (Coming Soon, Sold, Pending, etc.) were removed for clarity.
